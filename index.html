I am an incoming Ph.D. student at the University of Washington where I'll be working on problems in the intersection of natural language processing (NLP) and human-computer interaction (HCI). I am currently interested in the prospect of leveraging human computation to build large-scale high-quality datasets and to efficiently evaluate machine-generated text, both of which are necessary for training and testing robust large-scale NLP models. I am also interested in building interactive and human-in-the-loop NLP systems as well as using NLP effectively to understand large-scale qualitative data.

Previously, I was an AI Center Fellow at Microsoft Research, where I worked on identifying the disparity which exists between NLP research & its deployment and developing technologies to bridge this gap. I have also spent time pursuing research at Carnegie Mellon University and University College London, as well as two summers with Google Summer of Code working for Julia Computing and Mozilla.

Get in touch on if you want to discuss an idea or grab a (virtual?) coffee!
