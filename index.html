I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Washington where I work on problems in the intersection of natural language processing (NLP) and human-computer interaction (HCI). I am currently interested in the prospect of leveraging human computation to build large-scale high-quality datasets and to efficiently evaluate machine-generated text, both of which are necessary to train and test robust large-scale NLP models. I am also interested in building interactive and human-in-the-loop NLP systems as well as using NLP effectively to understand large-scale qualitative data.

I have previously worked/interned at Microsoft Research, Carnegie Mellon University, University College London, and Google Summer of Code (with Julia Computing and Mozilla).
